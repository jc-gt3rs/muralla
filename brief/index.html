Overview

What is GabAI-Basa?

GabAI-Basa (Reading Guide) is a browser-native Filipino reading companion targeting Filipino students (Grades 5–10) who struggle with reading fluency. It is a lightweight, browser-native AI study companion that simplifies, explains, reads, and rates pronunciation in English and Filipino — with no login, no install, and no setup.

Core challenge: Cognitive Accessibility — delivering a lightweight, browser-native AI study companion that simplifies, explains, reads, and rates pronunciation in English and Filipino.

The Four Tools

Four tools. One URL. No login.

Each tool handles a different part of reading — listening, pronouncing, understanding, and looking up words. Pick the one you need.

🔊
Basahin Mo
TTS Reader — /basahin
An optimized Text-to-Speech audio reader. Students paste or upload text and listen to it read in English and Filipino with sentence-level chunked playback, adjustable reading rate and pitch, session caching, and Opus OGG audio compression for low bandwidths.
Google Cloud TTS English & Filipino Speed control Low bandwidth
🎤
Samahan Mo Ako
Co-Reader + Pronunciation Rater — /samahan
An interactive guided reader displaying text word-by-word with high-contrast highlighted pills. Real-time on-device speech-to-text pronunciation scoring with visual feedback (Correct / Close / Incorrect). Supports OpenDyslexic font toggle, letter-spacing, and font-size controls. Comprehension word-drag game after each session.

A clean dictionary lookup for word definitions, types, IPA phonetics, and example sentences. Results are cached in localStorage — look up a word once, and the definition loads instantly next time, even offline.

User Guide

How to Use GabAI-Basa

GabAI-Basa is completely free and works directly from your browser. No login. No download. Just visit the website and choose the tool you need.

1. Basahin Mo — Read to Me
  • Go to the Basahin Mo page.
  • Paste any text you want to read.
  • Press Play to hear the text read aloud, sentence by sentence.
  • Adjust reading speed using the slider, or click on any sentence to jump to it.
2. Samahan Mo Ako — Read with Me
  • Go to the Samahan Mo page and paste your text.
  • The app shows the text word-by-word. Tap a word to hear it pronounced.
  • Turn on the Pronunciation Check (Mic) switch.
  • Read the word back and get instant color-coded feedback.
3. Anong Salita — Dictionary
  • Go to the Anong Salita page.
  • Type in the word you want to look up.
  • Get the definition, pronunciation, and example sentence.
  • Previously searched words work even offline.
4. Ipaliwanag Mo — Explain It
  • Go to the Ipaliwanag Mo page.
  • Paste the confusing text.
  • Select your Grade Level (4-6, 7-9, or 10-12).
  • Click Explain it simply — AI rewrites it in simpler words.
Accessibility Settings (Aa): In the top right corner of any page, click the "Aa" button to change font size, adjust spacing, or toggle the OpenDyslexic font.

Accessibility

Accessibility Rules — Non-Negotiable

Every design decision is tested first against: does this work for a student with dyslexia? These rules are baked into the codebase and cannot be overridden.

  • Minimum 17px body text, 18px on reading surfaces.
  • Pronunciation feedback uses color + icon together — never color alone (colorblind safety).
  • Word highlight uses a bold filled pill shape, not just underline or color.
  • No audio data ever leaves the device — Web Speech API is on-device only.
  • OpenDyslexic font must be toggleable at all times in the co-reader.
  • No modals, popups, or animations on reading surfaces — motion reserved for feedback only.
  • All interactive targets: minimum 44px touch area on mobile.

UN SDG #4 Alignment

Advancing Quality Education

GabAI-Basa is directly engineered to advance the UN's Sustainable Development Goal #4 — ensuring inclusive and equitable quality education and promoting lifelong learning opportunities for all.

Target 4.1 — Equitable & Quality Education

Regional students frequently fall behind due to a lack of learning materials. By introducing bilingual text-to-speech and AI content simplification in English and Filipino, we democratize access to quality study tools regardless of geography or socioeconomic background.

Target 4.5 — Eliminate Literacy Disparities

Traditional educational software widely overlooks neurodivergent learners. Our application places cognitive accessibility at the forefront — incorporating OpenDyslexic fonts, high-contrast visual pacing, and localized speech scoring to provide an equitable learning landscape for students with dyslexia and reading difficulties.

Target 4.a — Inclusive Learning Environments

We extend the concept of an inclusive "learning environment" into the digital realm. By building a Progressive Web App (PWA) with zero-bandwidth scoring and extreme data optimization, we ensure full functionality in remote, low-connectivity public schools across rural regions.

Known Constraints

MVP Limitations

Browser: Pronunciation rater requires Chrome or Edge (Web Speech API). Safari/Firefox: manual fallback, no scoring.
Input cap: Summarizer input capped at 3,000 characters (Gemini Flash rate limits during demo period).
Privacy: No user data stored. Mic access is session-only, never sent to server. No accounts, no tracking, no cookies.
